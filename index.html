🖼 Case 1 – Catch the Criminal! (Image Metadata OSINT)

A criminal on the run posted images online with the caption "I'm roaming free. You will never catch me." Law Enforcement has handed you the evidence images. Use EXIF metadata analysis to track them down.

Steps:
1. Upload the provided evidence images at onlineexifviewer.com
2. Analyse all metadata fields carefully
3. Answer all questions below — don't let the criminal run!
1 What is the camera model used to take the evidence photo?
2 When was the picture taken? (Mention exact Date & Time)
3 What are the GPS coordinates of the picture? (Exact Latitude & Longitude)
4 Did the flash fire on the camera when the photo was taken?
5 What does the comment embedded in the first image (Evidence_1) say?
6 What is the file size of Evidence_2 in bytes?
7 What is the file size of Evidence_1 in bytes?
8 Based on the GPS coordinates found, where could the criminal be? Name the location.

🕵 Case 3 – Find the Criminal's Bio (Advanced OSINT)

Police recovered an image from a suspect's laptop but couldn't identify them. As an OSINT investigator, you must trace the criminal's personal information and submit it to Law Enforcement.

Evidence Image:
sakurapwnedletter.svg (click to open)

Key Tools: Google  |  GitHub  |  Etherscan.io  |  TinEye  |  Google Lens  |  Google Images
1 What is the username of the criminal?
💡 View Page Source of the SVG file to find the embedded username.
2 What is the attacker's full real name?
💡 Enter the found username in the Google Images tab to reveal the real identity.
3 What is the full email address used by the attacker?
💡 Search the username on Google → navigate to their GitHub repos → use ChatGPT or decoding tools to extract the email address.
4 What cryptocurrency does the attacker own a wallet for?
💡 Navigate to the attacker's GitHub profile and search their repositories for any cryptocurrency-related repos.
5 What is the attacker's cryptocurrency wallet address?
💡 Look in the Commits tab of the relevant GitHub repo. The address starts with 0xa.
6 What mining pool did the attacker receive a payment from on January 23, 2021 UTC?
💡 Paste the wallet address into etherscan.io, filter by date, and click the January 23, 2021 transaction.
7 What other cryptocurrency did the attacker transfer using their wallet?
💡 On Etherscan, check the "To" tab of the wallet transactions to find transfers of a different coin.
8 What is the attacker's current Twitter/X handle?
💡 Search the criminal's name on Google and look for their Twitter/X profile link.
9 Is there any post on their Twitter/X account that mentions the Dark Web?
💡 Browse their tweets carefully. Answer with Yes or No.
10 Which airport is closest to the location where the attacker shared a photo before boarding a flight?
(Tweet contains the phrase: "before heading home!")
💡 Find that tweet → right-click image → "Search with Google Lens" → identify the place → Google "airport near [place name]".
11 What airport did the attacker have their last layover in?
💡 Find the "My final layover" tweet → right-click copy image address → paste in tineye.com → compare results with their posted images.
12 What lake can be seen in the map shared by the attacker?
💡 Right-click map image → Search with Google → crop the island area → get the island name → paste in Google Maps to find the nearby lake.
